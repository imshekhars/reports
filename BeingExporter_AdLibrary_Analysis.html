Executive Summary

One funnel, relentlessly repeated. Every single one of the 70 ads in the active library points to the same destination: a ₹199, 2-day live export workshop hosted by coach Bhagirath Goswami, with the URL 2DAYS.BEINGEXPORTER.COM. There is no product diversification — this is a single-offer info-product brand running a high-volume, performance-driven Meta funnel.
Video-first, mobile-first. 79% of active listings are video (mostly 20–60 seconds, formatted in vertical / Reels-friendly crops). Static ads exist but are a minority and seem to function as creative rotation rather than the core format.
Zero partnership ads detected. No ad uses the Meta "BeingExporter with [creator handle]" format. All ads are first-party brand ads. The brand does, however, lean heavily on named student testimonials (Mohit, Ashish, Ankit, Mahesh, Moiz, Mitul, Jignesh, Jasmin, Satyaveer) which simulate the social-proof effect of partnership ads without the partnership disclosure.

The five messaging pillars that show up again and again:
  1. Low barrier of entry — "You don't need a factory / inventory / huge investment / experience." This appears in roughly half of all ads and is the single most repeated objection-handler.
  2. Price anchor at ₹199 — explicitly stated in ~64% of ads. A deliberately tiny tripwire price for a paid front-end workshop.
  3. Mass social proof — "70,000+ exporters", "thousands have already started", "join the movement." Used to normalize the behaviour.
  4. Authority via Bhagirath Goswami — name-dropped in ~27% of ads, framed as "India's leading export coach."
  5. Mechanism reveal — almost every ad teases the same three deliverables: pick a product → find global buyers → start with the Merchant Export model.

Recommendations (synthesised observations)

  • Activate Branded Content / Partnership Ads. The brand already has rich testimonial assets — running them through the creator's own handle as partnership ads would unlock incremental reach and a trust angle the brand handle can't carry alone.
  • Lean into the proven evergreen hook. "India is exporting 11,000+ products globally — many with 40%+ margins" is the hook attached to the longest-running ads. Worth doubling down on as the primary cold-traffic creative.
  • Segment the persona testimonials. The Mohit / Ashish / Satyaveer arcs each speak to a different buyer (consultant, manufacturer, banker). With ad-set-level audiences these could be deployed as look-alike-targeted creatives rather than broad-targeted variants.
  • Audit static-ad ratio. Only 21% of the library is static — given how cheap statics are to produce, more aggressive static testing for thumb-stop and copy-only hooks could lower CPMs.
