How formats were identified:
Ads with a title repeated 3× (e.g., "Save ₹1 Lakh | Save ₹1 Lakh | Save ₹1 Lakh") are carousel ads — Meta's API returns each card's title concatenated. Ads with {{product.name}} are dynamic catalog ads (seen in historical/inactive set). All others are classified as single-format link ads.
Format Note

The carousel format is used exclusively for the Hindi market ("₹1 lakh savings" message). All English, Telugu, and Marathi ads use single-format link ads. This suggests the carousel performs better for Hindi-speaking audiences or that it's still being A/B tested. Dynamic catalog ads only appeared in the 2024 history and have since been dropped — likely a product listing experiment that didn't stick.

Overall Assessment

FreyrEnergy is running a textbook performance marketing playbook for a high-ticket residential product in India. Their approach — launch many variants simultaneously, let the algorithm optimise, anchor with specific financial numbers, drive to a free quote — is operationally sophisticated. The most interesting strategic signal right now is the EMI pivot happening today (June 1, 2026): shifting from "save ₹1 lakh over time" to "pay just ₹1,466/month" is a fundamentally different frame for the same product. If EMI messaging outperforms savings messaging in their tests, expect the next wave to be dominated by EMI copy across all languages. The addition of Marathi (Maharashtra) as a new market and the complete absence of Malayalam suggests they are actively reading their data and re-allocating budget to wherever acquisition is cheapest.
