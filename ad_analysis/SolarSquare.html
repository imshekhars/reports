🎬 1. Format Breakdown

Ad Format Distribution

Video Duration Breakdown

🎥 Video-First Strategy

SolarSquare runs 69% of ads as video, betting heavily on motion content to drive awareness and engagement. The majority (44%) are 30–60s "explainer-length" videos — long enough to educate but short enough to hold attention in-feed.

📸 Static for Reach

Static image ads (31%) are primarily used for link-click campaigns with FB.ME or direct landing page links. They typically carry the "Breaking News" visual treatment or government subsidy infographic format.

📲 Platforms

Ads run primarily across Facebook and Instagram. A subset extends to Messenger (Audience Network). No TikTok or YouTube placements visible in library data.

🤝 2. Brand Ads vs. Partnership Ads

Brand vs. Partnership Distribution

🔍 No Formal Creator Partnership Ads Detected

Across all 294 active ads analyzed, zero ads carry a "Paid Partnership with [creator]" label in the Ad Library. The Branded Content section of Meta's Ad Library — which would show creator-tagged partnership posts — returned no results for SolarSquare Energy's page ID.

What this means:

SolarSquare is running a 100% owned/brand media strategy. All ads are served directly from their brand page with no formal influencer partnerships declared through Meta's branded content system. Their creative does feature testimonial-style content and one verified customer story (Rajendra Garg), but these are produced in-house, not through creator partnerships.

💬 3. Core Messaging Strategies

Messaging Theme Frequency (of 294 active ads)

💰 Pillar 1: The "₹0 Entry Point"

The dominant narrative across 72+ ads is zero upfront investment. The formula: "Government subsidy covers your downpayment, your bill savings cover your EMI." This removes the #1 objection (cost) entirely. It's used in English, Hindi, Marathi, and Tamil.

🏛️ Pillar 2: Government Subsidy Urgency

109 ads (37%) explicitly mention the ₹1,08,000 government subsidy. SolarSquare uses this as a "money on the table" urgency trigger — framing inaction as leaving cash uncollected. The "Breaking News" creative hook (5 ads) also leads with this.

⚡ Pillar 3: 90% Bill Reduction

87 ads lead with the 90% electricity bill cut claim. Often paired with AC usage — "Run your AC all summer, forget the bill." This anchors the solar promise to an immediate, relatable pain point (high electricity bills).

📅 Pillar 4: Free Consultation Funnel

156 ads (53%) end with a "Book your FREE consultation" CTA. This is the universal conversion mechanism. "Get Quote" is the most common button (97 uses), followed by "Book Now" (116 combined).

🏆 Pillar 5: India's #1 Brand

20 ads explicitly claim "#1 solar company in India." Used primarily in Marathi and English to establish trust with fence-sitters. Paired with "30,000+ homes installed" social proof.

🌀 Pillar 6: Safety / WindPro™

9 ads (mostly Gujarati-targeted) lead with the WindPro™ mount — a cyclone-proof, 170 kmph-rated structure. This is a product differentiation play likely driven by Gujarat's coastal/storm exposure. It targets safety-conscious homeowners.

👥 4. Target Audience Personas

Inferred from ad copy themes, languages, geographic references, and emotional hooks — not from declared targeting data.

🏠 The Cost-Conscious Homeowner
Primary target · Hindi/Marathi/English ads · Urban & Tier-2 cities
●Middle-class homeowner aged 35–55 with high monthly electricity bills (₹5,000–₹15,000+), often running ACs in summer
●Primary fear: "Solar is too expensive upfront." Primary desire: cut the electricity bill without a large cash outlay
●Ads targeting them: "0 investment," "EMI covered by savings," "90% bill reduction" — entirely financial framing
●CTA: "Get Quote" or "Book a Free Consultation" — low-commitment entry
🌞 The Subsidy-Seeker
High-intent segment · Hindi/Marathi/Tamil/Telugu ads
●Already aware of PM Surya Ghar scheme. Wants to claim ₹1,08,000 subsidy but unsure how. Looking for a trusted executor
●Ads: "Breaking News — Turn your rooftop into a GOLDMINE" and "Earn up to ₹1,08,000 in government subsidy"
●Decision driver: speed + trustworthiness. SolarSquare positions itself as the safest hands to navigate the subsidy process
●Likely skews older (50+), owns home outright, financially cautious, responds to "official" / "news" framing
🌀 The Gujarat / Coastal Safety-Buyer
Regional niche · Gujarati-language ads
●Homeowner in cyclone-prone regions (Gujarat coast). Primary fear: roof damage during storm. Solar quality & structural safety is the deciding factor
●Ads: WindPro™ mount, "cyclone-proof," "withstands 170 kmph winds," "30,000+ homes trusted"
●Messaging is entirely differentiation-led — quality over savings — suggesting SolarSquare is going premium in this geo
●Likely upper-middle class, 40–60 years old, homeowner in city or peri-urban Gujarat
💼 The ROI-Driven Investor
Financial angle · English & Hindi
●Compares solar to financial instruments. Interested in the 24% return angle and ₹8/unit ROI framing
●Ads: "सोना बोझ बढ़ाए, सोलर दौलत बढ़ाए" (Gold adds burden, Solar builds wealth), "24% returns, lifetime bill savings"
●Likely dual-income household, financially literate, 35–50 years old, compares investment options
●A smaller but high-value segment — willing to invest more once the math is clear
